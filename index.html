The Strategy: Lean Bulk While Training for a Half Marathon

At 5'10", 69 kg, 28 years old — your TDEE is ~2,900–3,100 kcal. To gain lean mass while running high mileage, you need ~3,700–3,800 kcal/day with 290g+ protein. This is a body recomposition challenge: the running will burn fat while the 3,740 cal surplus builds muscle. Expect ~0.4–0.5 kg lean mass per week. The PPL split (Sun/Tue/Thu) is structured around your running days (Mon/Wed optional/Fri) so you never lift heavy the day before a key run. Saturday is active recovery — light movement, foam rolling, and mobility work. No full rest days.

Strength Programme
PPL — Week-by-Week Variation
Every week has different exercises to prevent adaptation, attack muscles from new angles, and stay mentally fresh. Core lifts (bench, row, squat) stay consistent — accessories rotate. Tap any week to see the full session.
⚡

How This Programme Is Built for Maximum Efficiency

Each week rotates accessory exercises while keeping the core compound lifts (Bench Press, Row, Squat, Deadlift) consistent so you can track progress. Supersets are used on antagonist muscles (push+pull, quad+hamstring) to cut session time by 20% with no loss in stimulus. Mechanical drop sets appear in Phases 2–3 on peak exercises. Injury-prevention moves (Nordic curl, tibialis raise, face pull, clamshell) rotate placement but never disappear.

Core compound — track weight every week
Superset pair — do back-to-back with 15s transition
Injury prevention — never skip regardless of time
New variation — different angle/stimulus vs last week
Drop set — reduce weight 20% after last set, rep to failure

Progression System
How to Progress Week to Week
The programme is useless without progressive overload. Follow this system exactly.
📈
Add reps first
If all prescribed reps feel easy, add 2 reps before adding weight next session.
🏋️
Then add weight
2.5 kg upper body, 5 kg lower body when you comfortably hit the top rep range.
📝
Track everything
Write weight and reps in your phone after every set. Non-negotiable. You cannot progress what you don't track.
⏰
Rest times matter
Compounds: 90–120 sec. Supersets: 60 sec after each pair. Isolation: 60 sec. Drop sets: none.
🚫
Don't ego lift before runs
Tue Pull is the day before Wed run. Thu Legs is the day before Fri long run. Heavy legs = slow run.
🔄
Why exercises rotate
Same exercise every week = accommodation. Different angle/exercise = new stimulus = continued growth. Core lifts stay, accessories rotate.

Outdoor vs Treadmill — How to Convert

Running on a treadmill is slightly easier because there's no wind resistance and the belt assists your stride. To match outdoor effort: set treadmill incline to 1% for easy runs, 1.5% for tempo, 2% for race pace intervals. Speed settings: 4:58/km = 12.1 km/h · 5:10/km = 11.6 km/h · 5:30/km = 10.9 km/h · 6:00/km = 10.0 km/h · 6:15/km = 9.6 km/h · 6:30/km = 9.2 km/h. Each run below shows both options.

Bulk vs Run: How to Fuel Both

Running and bulking seem contradictory — running burns calories, bulking requires a surplus. The solution: eat more on run days, slightly less on recovery days. On Mon/Wed/Fri (run days), add 1 extra scoop of whey or 200g extra rice to M2 or M4. On Saturday (active recovery), drop M5 snack or reduce by 50%. This keeps your weekly average at ~3,700 kcal while optimally fuelling each session type. Protein (293g) is the most important number — never miss it regardless of calorie adjustments.

The Golden Rule: First 5 km Must Feel Easy

Sub 1:45 is 4:58/km average. Most people blow up because they go out at 4:45/km in the first kilometre feeling fresh and pay for it at km 15. Your plan: go out at 5:05–5:10/km for the first 5 km. It will feel almost too slow. That's correct. Your race begins at km 10. Negative split = run the second half faster than the first half. This is how sub 1:45 is really won.

Race Split Strategy
DistanceTarget PaceCumulativeNotes
0–5 km5:05–5:10/km~25:30Feel easy. Let faster runners go. Your race starts at km 10. Relax arms, breathe controlled.
5–10 km5:00/km~50:30Find your rhythm. Breathing controlled. Relax arms. Take gel at km 10 if practiced in training.
10–15 km4:58/km~1:15:30This is race pace. Comfortably hard — you should be working. Don't look at overall time, only current km pace.
15–19 km4:55/km~1:35:00Dig in. Pump the arms. This is where sub 1:45 is won or lost. Tell yourself: "I've run this in training."

Race Morning Checklist
⏰
Wake 2.5 hrs before start
Eat familiar carbs — oats, toast, banana. Nothing new on race day. Ever.
💧
Hydrate smart
Sip 400–500 ml water with electrolytes over 2 hours. Don't gulp all at once.
🏃
Warm up properly
Arrive 40 min early. 8 min easy jog + 3×100m strides at race pace. Cold muscles = slow km 1.
👟
Wear what you trained in
Shoes, socks, shorts — all familiar. Race day is NOT the day for new gear or new socks.
🍬
Gel at km 10
Only if you've practiced this in a long run during training. Slow 3 sec to drink at aid stations properly.
📱
160–170 BPM playlist
Music at this tempo naturally lifts your cadence and matches ideal running stride rate.
🍝
Carb load Wed + Thu
Pasta, rice, bread on Wednesday and Thursday. No new foods. No spicy, no high-fibre, no alcohol.
😴
Sleep Thursday night most important
In bed by 9:30 PM Thursday. Friday complete rest. The pre-race night sleep actually matters less than you think.

Sub 1:45 is not just possible — with this plan it's the expected outcome.
Trust the Friday long runs. Don't skip Mondays. Eat your 293g protein every day.
