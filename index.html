The Strategy: Lean Bulk While Training for a Half Marathon

At 5'10", 69 kg, 28 years old — your TDEE is ~2,900–3,100 kcal. To gain lean mass while running high mileage, you need ~3,700–3,800 kcal/day with 290g+ protein. This is a body recomposition challenge: the running will burn fat while the 3,740 cal surplus builds muscle. Expect ~0.4–0.5 kg lean mass per week. The PPL split (Mon/Wed/Fri) is structured around your running days (Tue/Thu optional/Sat) so you never lift heavy the day before a key run.

Progression Rules
📈
Add reps first
If all reps feel easy, add 2 reps before adding weight. Hit top of range first.
🏋️
Then add weight
Add 2.5 kg upper body, 5 kg lower body when you comfortably max the rep range.
📝
Track every session
Write weights & reps every single time. You cannot progress what you don't track.
📉
Deload on cutback weeks
Weeks 3, 6, 10 — drop weight 10–15%. Your body gets stronger during recovery.
🚫
Don't ego lift before runs
Wed PULL before Tue run, Fri LEGS before Sat run — keep weights sensible or sore legs will ruin your run.
🍗
Protein every meal

Outdoor vs Treadmill — How to Convert

Running on a treadmill is slightly easier because there's no wind resistance and the belt assists your stride. To match outdoor effort: set treadmill incline to 1% for easy runs, 1.5% for tempo, 2% for race pace intervals. Speed settings: 4:58/km = 12.1 km/h · 5:10/km = 11.6 km/h · 5:30/km = 10.9 km/h · 6:00/km = 10.0 km/h · 6:15/km = 9.6 km/h · 6:30/km = 9.2 km/h. Each run below shows both options.

Bulk vs Run: How to Fuel Both

Running and bulking seem contradictory — running burns calories, bulking requires a surplus. The solution: eat more on run days, slightly less on rest days. On Tue/Thu/Sat (run days), add 1 extra scoop of whey or 200g extra rice to M2 or M4. On Sunday (full rest), drop M5 snack. This keeps your weekly average at ~3,700 kcal while optimally fuelling each session type. Protein (293g) is the most important number — never miss it regardless of calorie adjustments.

Race Day — Sub 1:45

Target pace: 4:58/km · Start conservative · Negative split · Empty the tank at km 19

🏁

The Golden Rule: First 5 km Must Feel Easy

Sub 1:45 is 4:58/km average. Most people blow up because they go out at 4:45/km in the first kilometre feeling fresh and pay for it at km 15. Your plan: go out at 5:05–5:10/km for the first 5 km. It will feel almost too slow. That's correct. Your race begins at km 10. Negative split = run the second half faster than the first half. This is how sub 1:45 is really won.
