M1 · Pre-Workout Fuel

⏰ 4:15 AM · Eat 30–40 min before training starts
Fast + slow carbs · light protein · no heaviness in stomach
~560 kcal 32g protein 72g carbs 18g fat
📝 Ingredients
Brown bread2 slices · ~60g
Peanut butter2 tbsp · ~32g
Banana1 large · ~120g
  • 1
    Prepare everything the night before: set bread, PB jar, and banana on the counter so you don't have to think at 4 AM.
  • 2
    Toast bread lightly (optional — untoasted saves 2 min and macros are identical).
  • 3
    Spread 1 tbsp PB per slice. Slice banana and place on top, or eat alongside as a whole.
  • 4
    Eat slowly over 10–12 min. Sip 300 ml water. Leave 25–30 min before starting warm-up.
⚡ Night Prep: Takes 3 minutes. Set everything out before sleeping so your 4 AM brain doesn't have to decide anything.
🔁 Quick Reference Macros
  • P
    Protein (32g): PB = 8g · Banana = 1g · Bread = 6g + ~17g from optional protein if added
  • C
    Carbs (72g): Bread = 30g (slow) · Banana = 27g (fast) · PB = 6g — perfect dual-speed fuel split
  • F
    Fat (18g): Almost entirely from PB. Healthy unsaturated fats — provide steady energy without bloating
  • !
    Timing rule: This meal must be eaten 30–45 min before training. Too close = nausea. Too far = energy dip mid-workout.

M2 · Post-Workout Anabolic Window

⏰ 6:30 AM · Consume within 30 min of finishing workout
Fast protein + fast + moderate carbs — most critical meal of the day
~580 kcal 55g protein 62g carbs 7g fat
📝 Ingredients — Whey + Oats
Whey protein1 scoop · ~30g
Full-fat milk300 ml
Oats (dry)60g
Water/milk for oats200 ml
Honey1 tsp (optional)
  • 1
    Prep night before: Measure whey into shaker. Keep it in the fridge with milk pre-poured. Shake = 5 seconds at 6:30 AM.
  • 2
    As you leave the gym or immediately after, shake the whey + milk. Drink while commuting home or immediately after workout.
  • 3
    Microwave 60g oats with 200 ml milk/water for 2 min. Stir at 1 min. Add 1 tsp honey for fast carb boost.
  • 4
    Consume both within 30 minutes of finishing your last set. Total window: 30 min post-workout maximum.
⏱️ 30-min window is critical. Muscle protein synthesis peaks here. Don't delay this meal. If commuting, pre-mix shake and drink in transit.
⚡ Why This Meal Matters
  • 1
    Protein timing: Muscles are insulin-sensitive post-workout. Fast protein (whey) hits the bloodstream in 30–45 min — optimal.
  • 2
    Carb role: Oats replenish glycogen stores depleted during training. This signals muscle to stop breaking down for energy.
  • 3
    Leucine trigger: Whey is the highest-leucine protein. Leucine directly activates mTOR — the primary muscle growth pathway.
  • 4
    If you skip this meal: The body remains in a catabolic state for 1–2 hours longer, eating away at muscle tissue you just worked to build.

M3 · Power Breakfast

⏰ 9:00 AM · After shower + commute
Sustained energy for work · continued muscle repair · gut health
~680 kcal 52g protein 72g carbs 22g fat
📝 Roti + Eggs + Rajma — Base Recipe
Whole wheat roti3 · ~90g
Eggs (half fry)3 eggs
Kidney beans / Rajma100g cooked
Oil1 tsp
Salt, pepper, cumin, turmericto taste
Lemon juice1 squeeze
  • 1
    Roti: Make dough the night before (store in fridge). Roll + cook 3 rotis on hot tawa, 1 min per side. ~6 min total.
  • 2
    Eggs: Heat 1 tsp oil in pan. Crack 3 eggs. Cover with lid for 2 min = set whites, soft yolk (half fry). Season with salt + pepper.
  • 3
    Rajma: Use pre-boiled batch. Reheat with cumin + turmeric + pinch of salt + squeeze of lemon, 3 min in small pan.
  • 4
    Serve together. Eat slowly. This is the first time today you should eat without rushing.
🗓️ Sunday Batch: Boil 500g dry rajma → ~1.5 kg cooked. Refrigerate. Breakfast ready in 8 min every morning all week.
⚡ Why 3 Sources Together
  • 1
    Eggs: Complete protein with all 9 essential amino acids. Yolk provides cholesterol for testosterone and Vitamin D.
  • 2
    Rajma: Slow-digesting plant protein + fiber. Keeps you full until lunch without blood sugar spikes.
  • 3
    Roti: Complex carbs from whole wheat provide steady glucose for 3–4 hours of work. Better than white bread here.
  • 4
    Combined effect: All three together hit all essential amino acids (complete protein profile) — critical for ongoing muscle repair from morning workout.

M4 · Mass Builder Lunch

⏰ 1:00 PM · Biggest calorie meal of the day
Max calories · max carbs + protein · office lunch
~720 kcal 52g protein 82g carbs 14g fat
📝 Grilled Chicken + Rice — Base Recipe
Chicken breast200g raw → ~160g cooked
White rice (dry)80g → 200g cooked
Ghee1 tsp on rice
Garlic + ginger paste1 tsp each
Spicescumin, coriander, turmeric, chilli
Lemon juice1 tbsp
Olive oil1 tsp for cooking
  • 1
    Marinate (Sunday): Mix chicken with garlic, ginger, cumin, coriander, turmeric, chilli, lemon, salt, oil. Overnight for best results.
  • 2
    Cook chicken: High pan heat. 5–6 min per side on medium-high. Rest 3 min. OR bake at 200°C for 22 min.
  • 3
    Cook rice: Rinse 80g dry rice. Simmer in 200 ml water + pinch of salt for 12 min covered. Rest 3 min. Fluff + add ghee.
  • 4
    Pack: 2-compartment lunchbox the night before. Microwave 2–3 min at work. This is your power meal.
📦 Batch Cook Sunday: Grill 600g chicken + cook 400g dry rice. Divide into 3 boxes (Mon–Wed lunches). Total cook time: 30 min. Saves 25+ min of daily cooking.
⚡ Why This Is the Biggest Meal
  • 1
    Timing: 1 PM is ~7 hrs after your workout. Muscle protein synthesis is still elevated — high protein meal here extends the anabolic window.
  • 2
    Insulin sensitivity: Midday insulin sensitivity is at its peak. This is the best time to eat the most carbs without fat storage.
  • 3
    Ghee on rice: The fat from ghee slows rice digestion slightly, preventing an energy crash at 2–3 PM.
  • 4
    Volume: At 720 kcal this is your largest meal. Don't try to eat it fast. Give yourself 15–20 min.

M5 · Evening Snack

⏰ 5:00 PM · Anti-catabolic bridge to dinner
Stop muscle breakdown · micronutrient load · satiety till 8 PM
~520 kcal 44g protein 42g carbs 20g fat
📝 Egg Whites + PB Toast + Veggies
Egg whites4 whites
Brown bread2 slices
Peanut butter2 tbsp
Broccoli25g
Cauliflower25g
Olive oil + lemon1 tsp + squeeze
  • 1
    Separate 4 egg whites into a bowl. Whisk with salt + pepper. Cook in non-stick pan — scrambled or thin omelette. 3–4 min.
  • 2
    Toast 2 slices of bread and spread 2 tbsp PB.
  • 3
    Steam broccoli + cauliflower — microwave with 2 tbsp water, covered, 3 min. Season with lemon + olive oil + black pepper.
  • 4
    Office version: Pack pre-boiled eggs, PB bread, veggies separately. No reheating needed for eggs or bread.
🏢 Office Desk: Keep a PB jar + bread + roasted chana in your desk drawer. Zero fridge needed. This snack never requires cooking if you use the desk-swap versions.
⚡ Why This Snack Matters
  • 1
    Anti-catabolic: 4 hours after lunch, blood amino acids drop. Without this snack, the body starts breaking down muscle for energy by 6–7 PM.
  • 2
    Why egg whites: Zero fat = fast absorption. Pure protein hit with no digestion load. Perfect mid-afternoon metabolism boost.
  • 3
    Veggies here: This is the best time to front-load micronutrients. Cruciferous vegetables reduce post-workout inflammation markers.
  • 4
    PB bread timing: Fat + slow carbs at 5 PM will digest over 2–3 hours, keeping you comfortable and not ravenous at 8 PM dinner.

M6 · Overnight Recovery Dinner

⏰ 8:00 PM · Last meal · focus on slow-release protein
Slow protein for 8-hr overnight repair · no fruits · sleep quality
~680 kcal 58g protein 60g carbs 22g fat
📝 Chicken Curry + Rice + Curd
Chicken breast/thigh200g
Brown rice (dry)~65g
Full-fat curd / dahi200g
Onion + tomato1 each
Ginger + garlic paste1 tsp each
Turmeric, cumin, coriander, garam masala½ tsp each
Oil1 tsp
  • 1
    Start rice first (25 min): Brown rice takes longer. Rinse, add to pot with 160 ml water. Bring to boil, reduce to lowest heat, cover 22 min, rest 3 min.
  • 2
    Curry: Sauté onion in 1 tsp oil until golden (5 min). Add ginger-garlic paste (2 min). Add tomato (4 min, until mushy).
  • 3
    Add all dry spices + salt. Stir 1 min. Add bite-sized chicken pieces. Mix to coat. Add 150 ml water, cover, cook medium-low 15 min.
  • 4
    Finish with garam masala + fresh coriander. Serve with rice + 200g curd on the side.
  • 5
    Before bed (~9:30 PM): Warm 200 ml full-fat milk + ½ tsp turmeric + pinch of black pepper. Sip slowly. Haldi doodh = casein + tryptophan for sleep quality.
🌙 Sleep tip: Finish dinner by 8:30 PM. Stop large water intake by 9:15 PM. Curd adds casein for slow 7–8 hour amino acid release while you sleep.
⚡ Overnight Muscle Science
  • 1
    Casein timing: Unlike whey, casein protein (in curd, paneer, milk) digests over 6–8 hours — feeding your muscles through the entire sleep period.
  • 2
    Why no fruits at night: Fruit sugar (fructose) is processed by the liver and can disrupt sleep architecture and fat storage patterns overnight.
  • 3
    Growth hormone: GH peaks during deep sleep (11 PM–2 AM). Having slow-release protein available in bloodstream at that time = maximum muscle synthesis during GH pulse.
  • 4
    Haldi doodh: Turmeric reduces overnight systemic inflammation. Tryptophan in milk converts to serotonin → melatonin = deeper sleep quality.

⚠️ Rules Before You Rotate

🚫 Never swap M1 + M2 carbs for low-GI Pre and post-workout carbs MUST be fast-digesting — banana, dates, white rice, white bread. Zero flexibility on this rule. High GI = fast insulin = protein into muscle.
✅ Rotate protein sources every day Chicken, fish, eggs, paneer, dal, mutton — each has a different amino acid + micronutrient profile. Variety across the week = better total muscle building stimulus.
🐟 Fish 2–3x per week is the #1 upgrade Omega-3 from fish reduces DOMS, improves sleep quality, and enhances muscle protein synthesis better than most supplements except creatine.
🗓️ Sunday meal prep = 90 min of work Cook 600g chicken/fish for Mon–Wed. Boil 2 dozen eggs. Batch rajma + rice. Portions into boxes. Makes all weekday swaps effortless at office.
🌱 Veg days on Thu + Sun rest days Lower activity means you don't need animal protein every meal. Paneer + dal + legume combinations cover all essential amino acids.
⚖️ How to adjust "slightly off" swaps When a swap has more fat (e.g. paneer), remove ghee from rice. When a swap has more carbs, reduce portion by 20%. Compensate in the same meal — don't recalculate everything.
🌙 No fruits at dinner (M6) Fructose in fruits is processed by the liver. Eating fruit at night can disrupt sleep architecture and shift overnight metabolism toward fat storage. Keep fruit to M1 and M3 only.
💧 Water timing matters too Don't drink 300+ ml right before or during a meal — it dilutes stomach acid and slows digestion. Drink 15–20 min before meals, and wait 20–30 min after. Sip during, don't gulp.
All swaps maintain ~±50 kcal & ±5g protein tolerance · Safe to rotate daily
